Date of World Pi Day

• History of Pi Day

In 1988, physicist Larry Shaw first recognized a pi (π) day. In 2009, the US House of Representatives designated March 14 as Pi Day. Later, UNESCO celebrated Pi Day as “International Mathematics Day” during its General Conference in 2019. Physicist Shaw linked the pi numbers with March 14 to organize a special day for the staff of the Exploratorium in San Francisco. The mathematician and physicist, Albert Einstein, known for the general theory of relativity, was born on Pi 3/14 in 1879. The value of pi was first calculated by a mathematician named Archimedes of Syracuse. It was later accepted by the scientific community when Leonhard Euler used the pi symbol in 1737. March 14 is also celebrated as "Pi Day" when the first 10 digits of pi were achieved at 9:26:53 a.m. in 2015 to form the exact moment Along with the date (3/14/15/92653). Pi is also part of Egyptian mythology, as people in Egypt believe that the pyramids of Giza were built on the principles of the number pi (pi). Every year, mathematics enthusiasts celebrate Pi Day with Pi party competitions and Pi Day exercises to stimulate interest in learning and practicing mathematics. Pi is calculated with over 50 trillion digits after the decimal point, it is an irrational number, infinitely long and its exact value cannot be known. Since the exact value of pi cannot be known, we can never find the exact area or circumference of a circle.

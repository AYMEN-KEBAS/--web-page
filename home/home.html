World "π" Day

• Pi Day is celebrated worldwide

Today, March 14, is International Mathematics Day and the Day of the Number Pi for the symbol π in mathematics, as this symbol is celebrated globally every year to recognize this mathematical constant. The Greek letter "π" is the symbol used in mathematics to represent a constant - the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter - which is 3.14159, which is approximately 3.14. According to the NDTV website, this day was chosen specifically because when this number is written in month/day format, it is (3/14) to match the first three digits of the mathematical constant.

The event takes place on this specific date because the first digits of “π” are 3, 1, and 4, so the celebration coincides with the 14th day of the third month of the year.

The celebration of World Mathematics Day includes many activities and events, such as lectures, workshops, competitions and exhibitions, that reflect the beauty and importance of mathematics. Mathematicians and educators also hope the symbol will help increase interest in mathematics and science through teaching, museum exhibits, competitions and more. Ultimately, the celebration of World Mathematics Day provides an opportunity to acknowledge the significant impact of mathematics on the development of our world and to inspire more individuals to pursue and value this vital and interesting subject.

In addition to honoring π, the celebration works to inspire more academic research and interest in mathematics and science.

Although the celebration is in honor of this sporting icon, many believe that it is an academic celebration, as schools, universities and museums around the world encourage this celebration.

Here are some fun facts about "π":

1- It is the sixteenth letter of the Greek alphabet.
2- It was proven to be an irrational number in the eighteenth century.
3- “π” Day falls on March 14, which is the birthday of the German genius, Albert Einstein.
4- It is impossible to know all the decimal numbers after the comma of the constant "π".
5- Using a powerful computer, Hitachi SR 8000, a Japanese scientist, discovered 1.24 trillion digits of “π,” breaking all previous records.
6- “π” appeared in many famous Hollywood films, as it was used as a secret code in “Hitchcock’s Torn Curtain” as well as in “The Net.”
